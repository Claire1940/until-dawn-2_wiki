<Section icon={Target} title="PC Optimization and Setup Steps">

Preparing your rig for a high-stakes horror game involves more than just meeting the raw numbers. You need to ensure your software environment is optimized to prevent crashes during critical Quick Time Events (QTEs).

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Update Drivers and OS">
    Always ensure you are running the latest "Game Ready" drivers from NVIDIA or AMD. For a 2027 title like this, Windows 11 is highly recommended due to its superior handling of DirectStorage and HDR.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Enable Resize BAR">
    Go into your BIOS and ensure Resize BAR (or Smart Access Memory for AMD) is enabled. This allows the CPU to access the entire GPU frame buffer, which is vital for the large assets used in cinematic games.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="SSD Optimization">
    Install the game on your fastest NVMe drive. Avoid using external HDDs or even SATA SSDs, as the sequel likely uses high-speed streaming to transition between character perspectives without loading screens.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure Upscaling">
    Given the heavy use of Ray Tracing, set your DLSS or FSR to "Quality" or "Balanced" mode. This will provide a significant FPS boost while maintaining the filmic look intended by the developers.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Performance Tip">
If you encounter micro-stutters, try disabling background recording software like Windows Game Bar or ShadowPlay, as these can conflict with the game's high CPU demands.
</Callout>

</Section>
