<Section icon={Shield} title="Choice and Consequence: The Butterfly Effect Returns">
The signature "Butterfly Effect" mechanic from the original Until Dawn makes a powerful return in **Until Dawn 2**, making player decisions more impactful than ever. Every choice, big or small, has the potential to ripple through the narrative, leading to unforeseen consequences and drastically altering the fate of the characters.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Relationship Dynamics">
    Managing relationships within the ghost hunter crew is crucial. Your interactions and decisions directly influence how characters perceive each other, opening or closing certain narrative paths. A strained relationship might prevent a character from helping another in a critical moment.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Moral Dilemmas">
    Players will face difficult ethical choices. Will you risk personal injury to save a creature, or prioritize the group's safety? These decisions are not always clear-cut and contribute significantly to the game's branching storylines.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Content vs. Survival">
    The crew's initial goal of creating viral content clashes with their desperate fight for survival. Decisions made for "the sake of content" under pressure from an intimidating boss can have dire, irreversible consequences for individuals or the entire group.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The narrative is designed to branch significantly based on these choices. Who survives until dawn, and who meets a grisly fate, is entirely in the player's hands. Even seemingly minor character moments can trigger a butterfly effect, leading to vastly different story outcomes.

| Decision Type          | Example Scenario                                | Potential Consequence                               | Impact Level |
| :--------------------- | :---------------------------------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- | :----------- |
| **Ethical Choice**     | Save a trapped animal vs. secure your own path  | Character trust, new path unlocked, or injury       | High         |
| **Relationship Choice**| Support a colleague vs. expose their secret     | Alliance formed, betrayal, or group conflict        | Medium       |
| **Survival Priority**  | Chase content vs. immediate escape              | Exposure to danger, missed escape window, character death | Critical     |
| **Quick Time Event**   | Succeeding vs. failing a critical action        | Injury, loss of item, or immediate death            | High         |

<Callout type="success" title="Enhanced Interactivity">
The developers have focused on making character relationships even more central to the branching narrative, ensuring that player choices regarding interpersonal dynamics have meaningful and visible impacts on the story.
</Callout>
