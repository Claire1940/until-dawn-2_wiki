<Section icon={Swords} title="Gameplay Mechanics and the Butterfly Effect">

At its heart, the **Until Dawn 2 remake** remains a choice-driven experience. The "Butterfly Effect" system, where small actions ripple into massive consequences, is the cornerstone of the gameplay. Every dialogue choice, every failed Quick Time Event (QTE), and every split-second decision can determine whether a character lives to see the sunrise or suffers a gruesome death.

Relationships within the ghost hunter crew are more dynamic than ever. Loyalty, romance, and betrayal are not just flavor text; they actively branch the story. A character who feels betrayed by the group might not help during a critical chase sequence later in the game.

**Key Gameplay Elements:**
- **Branching Narratives**: Thousands of possible paths based on player input.
- **Relationship Tracking**: Real-time updates on how crew members feel about each other.
- **High-Stakes QTEs**: Reflex-based moments that can result in immediate character death.
- **Exploration & Clues**: Finding environmental items to unlock "Premonitions" of future dangers.

| Mechanic | Description | Impact on Survival |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Butterfly Effect** | Small choices lead to big changes | **High** - Can cause late-game deaths |
| **Relationship Stats** | Tracks trust and tension | **Medium** - Affects help in crisis |
| **Premonitions** | Hidden items showing future deaths | **High** - Essential for avoiding traps |
| **Don't Move!** | DualSense sensor-based stillness | **High** - Critical for stealth scenes |
