<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Anticipated Gameplay Features & Butterfly Effect">
Until Dawn 2 is poised to deliver the same choice-driven horror that defined its predecessor. The developers, Firesprite, have emphasized the return of the "Butterfly Effect" system. This core mechanic ensures that every decision, no matter how small, can have far-reaching consequences on the story, character relationships, and ultimately, who survives.

### How the Butterfly Effect Shapes Survival

| Choice Impact        | Description                                                                  | Example Scenario (Hypothetical)                                | Potential Outcome                                                               |
|----------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Relationship Shift** | Small interactions can build or break trust between crew members.            | Choosing to save a character's gear over another's well-being. | The slighted character refuses to help in a later critical moment.               |
| **Immediate Danger**   | Direct actions that put characters in peril.                                 | Rushing through a dark corridor without checking for traps.    | Character suffers an injury, impacting their speed or ability to fight.         |
| **Information Gain**   | Decisions to explore or investigate can reveal crucial clues.                | Ignoring a strange noise to proceed quickly.                   | Missing a vital clue about the island's dangers, leading to a surprise attack. |
| **Moral Dilemma**      | Choices that test a character's ethics, often with no clear "good" answer.   | Deciding whether to sacrifice one character to save another.   | A character's death, or a survivor haunted by their decision.                   |
| **Survival Outcome**   | The cumulative effect of all choices determines who lives or dies.           | A series of poor relationship choices and reckless actions.    | All characters perish, leading to a "bad" ending.                               |
